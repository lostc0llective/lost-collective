## Chronology

- **17 September 1928.** City of Bankstown RSL sub-Branch founded by
  26 returned servicemen of the 1914 to 1918 war. Founding venue not
  recovered online. [S10][S9]
- **1955.** The Bankstown RSL clubhouse at 1 Meredith Street opens.
  Exact date and ceremony details not recovered online. [S1][S2]
- **Mid to late 20th century.** Club expands into a major social hub
  with live entertainment, fine dining, an underground swimming pool,
  squash court, gym and sauna. Membership peaks in the 1950s and 60s.
  [S1][S2]
- **1997.** Denis Phillips creates a memorial painting commemorating
  the First and Second World Wars, Malaya, Korea, Borneo and Vietnam.
  Installed beside the staircase with a plaque, AIF rising sun, an
  eternal flame, and a tropical fish tank below. [S8]
- **Early 2000s.** Club faces financial pressure, shifting social
  trends, and competition. Operations scale back; facilities are shed.
  [S2]
- **2017.** Club sells its land at 32 Kitchener Parade to Poly
  Australia to fund a new clubhouse and enable the wider
  redevelopment. By 2017 the club is operating on roughly a third of
  its original floor space. [S1]
- **2017 to 2019.** Brett Patman photographs the interiors of the
  1955 building before demolition. Documents the underground pool
  drained, the squash court's wooden floor bowed and peeling, blue
  walls, bright red ceilings. [S1]
- **15 January 2019.** Old Bankstown RSL clubhouse closes. [S1]
- **21 January 2019.** New Bankstown RSL & Hotel opens on the same
  site, designed by Altis Architecture (nominated architect Rolfe
  Latimer, NSW reg. 5535). [S1][S6]
- **March 2019.** Demolition of the 1955 clubhouse begins. [S1][S2]
- **April 2019.** Sydney South Planning Panel approves the Spring
  Square mixed-use redevelopment: 516 to 520 apartments across one
  14-storey and four 11-storey towers, retail, and a childcare
  centre. [S11]

## Architecture and site

- Located at 1 Meredith Street, Bankstown, with the wider development
  consolidating 23 and 27A Marion Street and 32 Kitchener Parade. The
  combined development site is 5,181 m² with frontages of about 65 m
  to Marion Street and 85 m to Meredith Street. [S5]
- Pre-demolition site (per the 2015 Development Application) comprised
  the main RSL Club building, a bowling green, and outdoor carparks.
  [S5]
- Opened 1955 in what Commercial Real Estate described as a building
  from the era "when kitsch was king", with interiors including
  "astonishing blue walls and bright red ceilings". [S1][S2]
- Underground sports facility: swimming pool, squash court, gym, and
  sauna, all original or near-original to the 1955 building (specific
  build dates not recovered). [S1]
- Architect of the 1955 building is unrecovered from public online
  sources. Flagged for R3 / offline (Bankstown Observer 1954 to 1956
  via Trove, NSW State Archives, Canterbury-Bankstown Library local
  history collection).

**Wording to use.** "Old Bankstown RSL" or "the 1955 Bankstown RSL
clubhouse" when distinguishing from the new club, which opened on the
same site in January 2019. "Returned and Services League" is the full
expansion of RSL. "Sub-Branch" with capital S and B is the convention
used by RSL NSW.

## Interiors and features (from Patman's documentation)

- Blue walls and bright red ceilings in at least one photographed
  space. [S1]
- Mid-century and later additions layered over decades of club use.
  [S1][S2]
- Underground swimming pool, drained at the time of photographing.
  [S1]
- Underground squash court with wooden floor bowed and peeling at the
  time of photographing. [S1]
- Underground gym and sauna. [S1]
- Live entertainment spaces, dining rooms, bars. [S2]
- WWI Honor Roll: a large carved-crown timber board with gold-script
  names of First World War servicemen, displayed in a Perspex case at
  the club entrance. Registered with the NSW War Memorials Register at
  the 32 Kitchener Parade address. Predates the 2019 demolition; must
  have been salvaged or relocated. [S7]
- Denis Phillips 1997 memorial painting: positioned beside the
  staircase, with plaque, AIF rising sun, eternal flame, and a
  tropical fish tank below. Commemorates the First World War, Second
  World War, Malaya, Korea, Borneo and Vietnam. Registered with the
  NSW War Memorials Register at the 32 Kitchener Parade address.
  Predates the 2019 demolition; must have been salvaged or relocated.
  [S8]

## Heritage status

- Bankstown RSL was NOT a listed heritage item under the Bankstown LEP
  2015, the planning instrument in force at the time of demolition.
  Confirmed by the absence of any heritage clause assessment in the
  Sydney West JRPP DA-1207/2015 assessment report; the assessment is
  run only against SEPP 55 (contamination), the Georges River REP, and
  the Bankstown LEP 2015 / DCP 2015 for bulk, scale, and FSR. A listed
  item would have triggered a heritage clause assessment, which is
  wholly absent. [S5]
- No record in the NSW State Heritage Inventory for "Bankstown RSL" at
  any of the constituent addresses. [S5]
- This explains why no Conservation Management Plan was commissioned
  for the demolition: the building was not statutorily protected.

## Role in wider context

- RSL clubs were a dominant feature of suburban Australian social life
  from the mid 20th century. Bankstown RSL's trajectory, from a 1928
  sub-Branch through to a major 1955 clubhouse, through late 20th
  century expansion, through financial pressure in the early 2000s,
  through sale and redevelopment in the 2010s, tracks the arc of the
  RSL club as a building type. [S1][S2]
- Paul Keating, who grew up in Bankstown and served as Australian
  Prime Minister 1991 to 1996, cited the Bankstown RSL Club, alongside
  Bankstown Sports, as a "great place of congregation" reflecting the
  area's social and cultural milieu. Said in his Bankstown City Silver
  Jubilee address, 27 May 2005. [S12]
- The City of Bankstown RSL sub-Branch operates as a separately
  registered charity governed by RSL NSW, distinct from the Bankstown
  RSL Club company. [S3][S9]

## Redevelopment and current use

- Developer: Poly Australia. [S1][S2]
- Replacement clubhouse: new Bankstown RSL & Hotel opened 21 January
  2019. Designed by Altis Architecture (Rolfe Latimer, NSW reg. 5535).
  [S1][S6]
- Spring Square mixed-use redevelopment, approved by the Sydney South
  Planning Panel April 2019: 516 to 520 apartments across one
  14-storey tower and four 11-storey towers, plus retail and a
  childcare centre. [S11]
- Patman's photographs were planned to feature in an exhibition in
  Poly's nearby display showroom and find a permanent home in the new
  Bankstown RSL Club, per the Commercial Real Estate feature. Whether
  they were physically hung in the new club post-January 2019 is not
  confirmed online; flagged for direct confirmation by Brett. [S1][S2]

## Narrative themes and angles

Use these as scaffolding for copy across formats. Each angle ties back
to at least one verified fact.

1. **1928 to 2019, ninety-one years.** The sub-Branch was founded on
   17 September 1928 by 26 returned World War I servicemen; the 1955
   clubhouse stood 64 years before demolition in March 2019. The
   sub-Branch and a new clubhouse continue. [S10][S1][S2]
2. **Kitsch is the right word.** Commercial Real Estate's own framing
   of the interiors gives permission to use "kitsch" as an editorial
   descriptor. Blue walls. Red ceilings. Wood-panelled bars. [S1]
3. **Last look before demolition.** Patman photographed the building
   intact, before the March 2019 demolition. This is the record that
   remains. [S1][S2]
4. **End of the suburban RSL era.** Bankstown RSL's story is a
   specific case of a broader Australian trend, one that Paul Keating
   also reaches for when describing the cultural fabric of the suburb
   he grew up in. [S2][S12]
5. **Underground.** Pool, squash court, gym, sauna, all built below
   ground. The pool drained, the squash court's floor bowed and
   peeling. [S1]
6. **What survives.** The First World War Honor Roll and the 1997
   Denis Phillips memorial both predate the 2019 demolition. They are
   on the NSW War Memorials Register at the 32 Kitchener Parade
   address. They had to be moved to remain registered. [S7][S8]

**Angles to avoid.** Do not imply the RSL organisation has
disappeared: a new Bankstown RSL operates on the same site and the
sub-Branch is still active. Do not romanticise the building as
untouched or ancient: 1955 is mid-century, and the interiors had been
remodelled through its life. Do not call it heritage-listed: it
wasn't.
