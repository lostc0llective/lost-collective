## Outreach email

**Subject:** Trade pricing for Lost Collective — fine art photography prints

---

Hi [Name],

I'm reaching out because your work comes up regularly when people talk about interiors that are considered rather than assembled — and that's the kind of client who tends to respond to Lost Collective prints.

Lost Collective is a Sydney-based fine art photography studio. The work documents Australian and Japanese industrial heritage — power stations, hospitals, factories, heritage hotels — shot in large format and printed on Ilford Galerie cotton rag or metallic glass at sizes from A4 to A1. Limited editions, each signed. The subject matter is specific: places with history, buildings that carried weight.

The prints work in commercial and hospitality settings because of scale and restraint. A 1189 x 841mm print of the White Bay Power Station turbine hall reads at double-height. The monochromatic palette sits across concrete, raw timber, and warm material palettes without competing. Several of the series — White Bay, Eveleigh, Halvorsens, Callan Park — are Sydney-specific enough to carry local resonance for clients who know the buildings.

Trade pricing is available for interior designers, architects, and fitout practices. Typically 20% off the published price, with lead times of 3–5 business days per print from order confirmation. I can supply high-resolution files for presentation to clients, produce test prints at cost, and work to specification on framing finish (raw, white, or black timber).

The full catalogue is at lostcollective.com. The trade page is at lostcollective.com/pages/trade.

Happy to send a physical sample to your studio if that's useful.

Brett Patman
Lost Collective
brett@lostcollective.com.au

## Notes on use

- Personalise the first paragraph with a specific project reference where possible
- For hospitality-focused firms, lead with White Bay, Eveleigh, Callan Park, Morwell — scale and industrial subject matter
- For residential-focused firms, lead with Halvorsens, Terminus Hotel, Landscapes series — warmer subject matter
- Physical samples are high-conversion — offer them to any firm that responds positively
- Follow up once, 10 days after first contact, with a specific image reference based on their recent work
