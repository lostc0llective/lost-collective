# Lost Collective — Shopify Content Brief for Claude Code

## What this document is

This is the complete content brief for all written content on the Lost Collective Shopify store (lostcollective.com). It covers every text field you will be asked to write or edit: collection descriptions, product descriptions, meta titles, meta descriptions, URL handles, and image alt text.

Follow this document for all Shopify content tasks. For the full tone of voice model, refer to `lost-collective-tone-of-voice.md` in this project.

---

## Voice summary

Write like a tradesman telling a smart friend about a place he visited, what he found there, and why it matters. Specific, confident, grounded. No inflated language. No emotional instructions to the reader. Let the facts and the detail do the work.

**The website is about the places, not the photographer.** No first person. No "I," "my," or "we" anywhere in Shopify content. Brett does not exist on the store. The only exception is the About page.

---

## Australian English

Use Australian spelling throughout. Colour, metre, centre, labour, recognised, specialised. Numerals for all numbers and measurements (5, not five; 100 years, not a hundred years).

---

## Banned language

Never use any of the following in any Shopify content field:

### Words and phrases
- delve, groundbreaking, foster, elevate, unleash, synergy
- hauntingly beautiful, evocative, captivating, timeless
- nestled, tucked away, hidden gem
- step back in time, frozen in time, time stood still
- rich tapestry, echoes of the past, whispers of history
- testament to, a reminder that, speaks to
- curated, crafted, meticulously, bespoke, handcrafted, artisanal
- explore, discover, uncover (as calls to action)
- transform your space, bring a unique touch
- preserving for future generations
- "each piece tells a story" or any variation
- "more than just a [thing]"
- "it's a bridge to the past"
- "this is that story"

### Calls to action to avoid
- "Explore the collection"
- "Discover the series"
- "Shop now"
- "Transform your space"

### Formatting
- No em dashes. Use commas, full stops, or restructure the sentence.
- No dramatic one-liner sign-offs.
- No sentences that tell the reader how to feel instead of giving them the detail to feel it themselves.

## Collection descriptions

These appear on Shopify collection pages. They are the primary text a visitor reads when they land on a location's page.

### Rules

1. **The place is the only subject.** No first person. No photographer's perspective. No "captured by" or "this series explores." The text is about the building, its history, and its significance.
2. **Open with what the place is and when it operated.** Ground the reader immediately. "White Bay Power Station operated from 1917 to 1983, supplying electricity to Sydney's inner west for over 6 decades."
3. **Be specific.** Name machinery, materials, architectural features, streets, suburbs. If the building had Babcock and Wilcox boilers, say so. If the ceiling is pressed metal, say pressed metal.
4. **Weave history naturally.** Dates, names, and facts should sit inside the narrative, not be delivered as a lecture. Drop them in where they serve the story.
5. **Keep under 150 words** unless the place genuinely warrants more. Most don't.
6. **Let the photos carry the emotion.** The text provides context, not atmosphere. Do not describe the mood of the photos.
7. **Centre the people.** If workers, patients, guests, or residents used this place, mention them and what they did there. The buildings matter because of the people.

### SEO for collection descriptions

- Naturally include the full location name (e.g., "White Bay Power Station," "Kinugawa Kan," "Terminus Hotel Pyrmont").
- Include "photography" or "wall art prints" where they read naturally.
- Include relevant geographic terms (Sydney, NSW, Japan, etc.) and architectural/industrial terms where appropriate.
- Never stuff keywords. If a keyword doesn't fit naturally in the sentence, leave it out.

### Example (calibration)

> White Bay Power Station operated from 1917 to 1983, supplying electricity to Sydney's inner west for over 6 decades. At peak capacity, the station's 6 turbine generators produced 186MW of power. The main turbine hall, boiler house, and control room remain largely intact. Workers entered through the Mullens Street gate and clocked on in shifts that ran around the clock. The station closed when newer facilities at Munmorah and Vales Point came online, and it has sat empty since. It is heritage listed and owned by the NSW Government.

This works because it is factual, specific, grounded in human detail (the workers, the gate, the shifts), and it does not try to make the reader feel something.

## Product descriptions

These appear on individual print product pages. They describe what the buyer is looking at in the photograph.

### Rules

1. **Describe what is in the photo.** The room, the feature, the light, the materials. Be literal and specific.
2. **One line of context** about where this space sits within the broader building.
3. **2 to 4 sentences maximum.** No more.
4. **No first person.** No photographer. No "captured in natural light" or "this image showcases."
5. **No telling the buyer how to feel.** Do not say "a haunting reminder of" or "a striking contrast between." Just describe the space.
6. **No sales language.** Do not mention print quality, framing options, paper stock, or shipping in the description. That information lives elsewhere on the page.

### SEO for product descriptions

- Include the location name naturally.
- Include the specific room or space name if it has one (e.g., "the main turbine hall," "the men's bar," "room 6").
- Include "print" or "wall art" once, naturally, only if it fits.

### Example (calibration)

> The main turbine hall viewed from the eastern gallery. Six turbine generators sit in a row along the hall floor, each still connected to its overhead crane rail. Morning light enters through the south-facing windows and catches the dust suspended in the air. This was the operational heart of White Bay Power Station for over 60 years.

This works because the reader can see the photo in their mind before they even look at it. No emotion, no sales pitch, just the space.

## Meta titles

These appear in search engine results and browser tabs. Format differs by page type.

### Collection pages

**Format:** [Location Name] Wall Art Prints | Lost Collective

**Examples:**
- White Bay Power Station Wall Art Prints | Lost Collective
- Kinugawa Kan Wall Art Prints | Lost Collective
- Terminus Hotel Pyrmont Wall Art Prints | Lost Collective
- Callan Park Wall Art Prints | Lost Collective

**Rules:**
- Use the full, commonly known name of the location.
- Always include "Wall Art Prints" for commercial intent.
- Keep under 60 characters where possible (Google truncates after roughly 60).
- If the location name is long, abbreviate sensibly: "Shimizusawa Thermal Power Plant Prints | Lost Collective" (drop "Wall Art" before dropping the location name).

### Product pages

**Format:** [Print Name or Description] | [Location Name] | Lost Collective

**Examples:**
- Turbine Hall | White Bay Power Station | Lost Collective
- Room 6 | Kinugawa Kan | Lost Collective
- The Men's Bar | Terminus Hotel | Lost Collective

**Rules:**
- Lead with the specific space or print title.
- Include the location name for context and SEO.
- Keep under 60 characters. If too long, drop "| Lost Collective" last (it's the least important for search).

### Blog posts

**Format:** [Compelling, specific title including location name] | Lost Collective

**Rules:**
- The title should be specific and intriguing, not clickbait.
- Include the location name in the first 5 words where possible.
- Do not use banned phrases in titles.

## Meta descriptions

These appear below the title in search engine results. They are the pitch that gets someone to click.

### Rules

1. **Under 160 characters.** Google truncates after this.
2. **Specific to the content.** Not generic. Not reusable across pages.
3. **Include the location name.**
4. **State a fact or detail that makes someone want to see the photos.** The goal is curiosity, not hype.
5. **No banned language.** No "explore," "discover," "hidden gem," "step back in time."
6. **No calls to action** like "Shop now" or "Browse the collection."
7. **No first person.**

### Examples (calibration)

**Collection — White Bay Power Station:**
> Sydney's longest-serving power station produced 186MW from 1917 to 1983. Fine art photography prints of the turbine hall, boiler house, and control room.

**Collection — Terminus Hotel:**
> A blue-collar pub in Pyrmont where quarry workers and wharfies drank after shifts. Photography prints of the hotel's bars, rooms, and ivy-covered facade.

**Collection — Kinugawa Kan:**
> A resort hotel in Tochigi Prefecture, Japan, closed since the early 2000s. Photography prints of guest rooms, banquet halls, and the main lobby.

**Product — Turbine Hall (White Bay):**
> Six turbine generators in a row, each still connected to its overhead crane rail. Morning light through the south-facing windows. White Bay Power Station.

These work because they give the searcher a reason to click that is grounded in a specific, interesting fact. Not a sales pitch.

## URL handles

These are the slugs in the Shopify URL (e.g., lostcollective.com/collections/white-bay-power-station).

### Rules

1. **Lowercase, hyphenated, no special characters.**
2. **Use the location name as the primary handle.** Keep it short and clean.
3. **Do not include "collection" or "prints" in the handle.** Shopify already puts /collections/ in the path.
4. **Be consistent with naming across the store.**

### Examples

- white-bay-power-station
- terminus-hotel
- kinugawa-kan
- callan-park
- hotel-motel-101
- streetscapes-of-yubari
- shimizusawa-thermal-power-plant

For product handles, use the format: [location]-[space-or-print-name]
- white-bay-turbine-hall
- terminus-hotel-mens-bar
- kinugawa-kan-room-6

## Image alt text

Alt text serves 2 purposes: accessibility (screen readers) and SEO (Google image search). Both require the same thing: describe what is actually in the image.

### Rules

1. **Describe what is in the photograph.** The room, the machinery, the architectural feature, the light. Be literal.
2. **Include the location name.**
3. **Do not describe the mood or emotion.** No "hauntingly beautiful photograph of" or "atmospheric shot of." Just the content.
4. **Keep under 125 characters.** Screen readers cut off after this.
5. **No keyword stuffing.** One mention of the location name is enough.
6. **Do not start with "Photo of" or "Image of."** Screen readers already announce it as an image.

### Examples

- Turbine hall at White Bay Power Station with six generators and overhead crane rails
- Ivy-covered facade of the Terminus Hotel, Pyrmont, Sydney
- Abandoned guest room at Kinugawa Kan with tatami mats and peeling wallpaper
- Boiler house interior at Wangi Power Station, Lake Macquarie
- Neon motel sign at night, Hotel Motel 101 series
- Empty banquet hall at Kinugawa Kan, Tochigi Prefecture, Japan

## The checklist

Before finalising any Shopify content, verify:

1. No first person anywhere (no I, my, we, our).
2. No banned words or phrases.
3. No em dashes.
4. Australian English spelling.
5. Numerals for all numbers.
6. Location name appears naturally at least once.
7. The text is about the place and the people, not the photos or the photographer.
8. No sentence tells the reader how to feel.
9. Every sentence earns its place. If you can remove one and lose nothing, remove it.
10. Meta title under 60 characters.
11. Meta description under 160 characters.
12. Alt text under 125 characters, literal, no mood language.
